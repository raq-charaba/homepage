About this project

A Power BI dashboard modeling Microsoft Copilot adoption across six departments over a six month period. The project tracks license usage, productivity gains, and governance performance, helping leadership evaluate AI program results and identify areas requiring attention before broader deployment.

Live dashboard

Copilot Readiness & ROI Tracker — Power BI

Executive Summary

As of December 2024, Copilot adoption across the organization has reached an average of 56% — with Sales leading at 93% and Operations lagging at 50%. Active licenses are generating an estimated $159K in cumulative productivity value, driven primarily by Code Assistance (749 hours saved) and Data Analysis (590 hours saved).

However, $15K per month in license spend remains wasted due to inactive users — concentrated in Operations ($2.7K/mo) and Engineering ($3.0K/mo). Closing this gap through targeted enablement programs represents the highest-leverage near-term opportunity.

On governance readiness, Sales and Marketing are fully compliant across all five pillars. Operations remains the only department rated Not Ready, with DLP Policies and User Training both scoring below 55. Scaling adoption in Operations without first addressing these gaps poses a compliance risk.

Recommended actions: prioritize Operations enablement, set an org-wide 80% adoption target by Q1 2025, and mandate DLP policy completion before expanding Copilot access to at-risk departments.

Live dashboard

M365 Usage & Adoption Health Monitor — Power BI

Executive summary

As of December 2024, the organization achieved an M365 Health Score of 74.1, placing it just below the Healthy benchmark. Outlook (95.8%) and Teams (86.3%) show strong adoption and provide a solid foundation for collaboration, while lower usage of SharePoint (56.8%), Word (53.7%), and PowerPoint (48.9%) highlights opportunities to improve digital workplace maturity.

SharePoint emerged as a key success area, growing 12.5% month over month and indicating increasing comfort with cloud-based document management. In contrast, PowerPoint adoption remained below target with minimal growth, limiting opportunities to leverage AI-driven productivity enhancements.

Department-level analysis identified Operations as the primary area of concern, maintaining a Health Score of 57.5 for six consecutive months. Based on these findings, I recommended targeted SharePoint and PowerPoint adoption initiatives, along with a focused improvement plan for Operations before expanding Copilot licensing across the department.

About this project

This Power BI dashboard was designed to help leaders monitor workforce sentiment across Sales, Operations, Marketing, Customer Success, and Engineering. Using six months of simulated employee survey responses, it consolidates team-level sentiment scores, negative feedback rates, response volume, and month-over-month movement into an executive-ready reporting experience.

The solution pairs high-level KPI tracking with deeper diagnostic views. Leaders can compare sentiment trends by team, review sentiment distribution, inspect negative open-text feedback with confidence scores, and identify when a team falls below the alert threshold. The Alert Center extends the reporting workflow by outlining how Power Automate, AI Builder, SharePoint, and Outlook can be used to notify managers and log intervention events.

Live dashboard

Workforce Sentiment & Productivity Pulse - Power BI

Executive summary

As of December 2024, the workforce sentiment program captured 868 employee responses with an average sentiment score of 3.75. Positive sentiment represented 53.34% of responses, while neutral sentiment accounted for 30.99% and negative sentiment reached 15.67%, creating a clear baseline for monitoring employee experience across teams.

Sales and Customer Success ended the period as the strongest performers, reaching December sentiment scores of 4.43 and 4.16 respectively. Engineering recovered to 3.69 after an October dip, while Operations remained the lowest-scoring team at 2.66 and continued to require targeted leadership attention.

The Alert Center identified two threshold events in October: Operations at 2.21 and Engineering at 2.34, both below the 2.5 alert threshold. The dashboard recommends manager follow-up through skip-level meetings, workload distribution reviews, and continued monitoring of negative feedback themes such as resource gaps and inconsistent communication.

Recommended actions: prioritize Operations and Engineering pulse reviews, investigate workload and communication drivers, and use the automated Power Automate alert pipeline to ensure future low-score events are routed, logged, and addressed quickly.
